    Katrina Elam Singer

    Katrina moved to Nashville, Tennessee, after she finished high school, where she obtained a contract with Universal South Records. Jimmie Lee Sloas produced her first self-titled album, unleashed on Oc toner 5, 2004. The album reached No. 42 on Billboard’s Top Country Albums chart and No. 33 on Top Heatseekers chart.

    The first single, ”No End Sight”, reached No. 29 on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart. The follow-up single ”I Wanty a Cowboy” reached No. 59 on the same chart. Katrina also toured in 2004 with Keith Urban. Katrina later toured with Rascal Flatts to promote her unreleased second album, Turn Me Up.

    ”Love Is,” the first single released from the album, peaked at No. 47 on Billboard’s Hot Country Songs chart in 2006. Another single from the album, ”Flat on the Floor,” finally peaked at No. 52. Later in the year, Carrie Underwood includes a version of the track on her 2007 album Carnival Ride. Katrina left Universal South in 2008. She also c0-wrote the track ”Change” on Underwood’s 2009 album Play On.

    Reba McEntire covered ”I Want a Cowboy” on her 2009 album Keep on Loving You. Katrina also co-wrote Rascal Flatts’ 2011 single ”Easy”, which features Natasha Bedingfield, and Eli Yong Band’s 2012 single ”Say Goodnight”, co-written by John Paul White of The Civil Wars. Hunter Hayes co-wrote his single ”Invisible” in 2014 with Katrina.

    Katrina was cast in a continuation in late 2010 to the 1992 film Pure Country, titled Pure Country 2: The Gift, Katrina’s ”Dream Big” was unleashed to radio in late 2010 and served as the lead single for the movie’s soundtrack, which was unleashed on February 8, 2011.

    Katrina Elam Albums

    The Katrina: Elam album is a self-titled debut album of American country music artist Katrina Elam. It was released in 2004 (see 2004 in country music) on the Universal South Records label. The album peaked at #42 on the Billboard Top Country Albums charts and #33 on Top Heatseekers.

    It produced two singles in “No End in Sight” and “I Want a Cowboy”, which respectively reached #29 and #59 on the Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now Hot Country Songs) charts. After the release of Katrina Elam, Elam recorded a second album for Universal South in 2007, entitled Turn Me Up. Although two of this album’s tracks charted, the album itself was not released, and Elam exited Universal South’s roster.
