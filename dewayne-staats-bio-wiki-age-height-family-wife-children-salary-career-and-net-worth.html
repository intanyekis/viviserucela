    Career

    Dewayne Staats is a sportscaster who has been the television play-by-play commentator for the Tampa Bay Rays since their inception in 1998. He is currently working with color commentator Brian Anderson. He started his broadcasting career in 1973, while still a student at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville, broadcasting games for the Oklahoma City 89ers minor league baseball team.

    After graduating, he was employed at KPLR-TV in St. Louis as a sports director before moving on to ESPN (1995-97), New York Yankees (1990-94), Houston Astros (1977-84), and Chicago Cubs (1985-89).

    Notable Travels, Events, and Experiences

    • Travels: Staats has traveled to many different countries to call baseball games. He has traveled to many countries like Korea, the Dominican Republic, Japan, and Mexico. He has also called games in all 30 MLB stadiums.

    • Events: Staats announced the 1998 World Series, when the New York Yankees defeated the San Diego Padres, the 2007 World Series when the Boston Red Sox defeated the Colorado Rockies, and the 2017 World Series when the Houston Astros defeated the Los Angeles Dodgers. He also announced the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang, South Korea, and the 2022 Super Bowl when the Los Angeles Rams defeated the Cincinnati Bengals.
    • Experiences: Staats has had the chance to meet and interview many well-known athletes, including Willie Mays, Derek Jeter, Mickey Mantle, and Hank Aaron. He has also interviewed presidents and other world leaders.
