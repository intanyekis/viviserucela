    Gary Trent Jr. is a young and talented basketball player who has quickly become a rising star in the NBA. Born on January 18, 1999, in Columbus, Ohio, Trent Jr. grew up in a basketball family. His father, Gary Trent Sr., played nine seasons in the NBA and had a successful career, which made an impact on his son’s love for basketball.

    Trent Jr. began playing basketball at a young age and quickly developed a passion for the game. He attended Apple Valley High School in Minnesota, where he became one of the top high school basketball players in the country. Trent Jr. was a McDonald’s All-American, a Jordan Brand Classic participant, and was named Minnesota’s Mr. Basketball in 2017.

    After high school, Trent Jr. committed to playing college basketball at Duke University, one of the most prestigious college basketball programs in the country. During his freshman season, Trent Jr. was a key player on a Duke team that included future NBA stars Zion Williamson, RJ Barrett, and Cam Reddish. He averaged 14.5 points per game and helped lead Duke to the Elite Eight of the NCAA Tournament.
