    Glossary of Common Scams

    Crypto scams have become increasingly prevalent in the digital world, making it crucial for individuals to be aware of the various scam types to protect themselves from fraud and financial loss. Here is a glossary of common scams to look out for:

  • Email Phishing Scams: Scammers impersonate legitimate organizations and send fraudulent emails with the intention of obtaining personal information or financial details.
  • Fraudulent Trading Platforms: These platforms claim to provide investment opportunities in cryptocurrencies but are actually designed to deceive individuals and steal their funds.
  • Imposters: Scammers pretend to be trustworthy individuals or organizations in order to gain trust and manipulate their victims into handing over money or sensitive information.
  • Advance Fee Scams: Scammers request upfront payment or fees for promised services or financial gains that they never deliver, leaving victims with financial loss and disappointment.

    • To protect yourself from falling victim to these scams, it is crucial to research and verify the credibility of companies and websites before investing or engaging in any financial transactions. Additionally, always be cautious when sharing personal or financial information online and report any suspicious activities to the relevant authorities.

    Remember, staying informed is the key to staying safe in the crypto world.

    Consumer Complaints and Scams

    The Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI) has received numerous consumer complaints regarding scams associated with crypto transactions. These scams encompass a range of fraudulent activities, including phishing scams, deceptive trading platforms, and asset recovery scams.

    Victims of these scams have reported significant financial losses resulting from operations such as website account closures, fees imposed for claiming assets, and fraudulent platforms freezing accounts. Additionally, victims have encountered unexpected taxes and other deceptive practices. It is crucial for individuals to remain informed about these scams and promptly report any suspicious activities to protect others from falling victim to similar fraudulent schemes.
